<p><ul><li>Pure Biodiesel Fuel</li><li>Non-pure Biodiesel Fuel</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biodiesel fuel market consists of two main types: pure biodiesel fuel and non-pure biodiesel fuel. Pure biodiesel, often referred to as B100, is made entirely from renewable resources like vegetable oils or animal fats, offering a cleaner alternative to conventional diesel. Non-pure biodiesel, typically a blend (such as B20, which contains 20% biodiesel and 80% traditional diesel), combines fossil fuels with biodiesel to enhance emissions performance while maintaining compatibility with existing diesel engines.</p></p>
