<p><ul><li>Physical Adsorption Hydrogen Storage</li><li>Chemical Hydrogen Storage</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The solid hydrogen storage material market includes two primary types: physical adsorption and chemical hydrogen storage. Physical adsorption involves storing hydrogen on the surface of materials like activated carbon or metal-organic frameworks, leveraging weak van der Waals forces for efficient storage. In contrast, chemical hydrogen storage entails forming chemical bonds between hydrogen and storage materials, such as metal hydrides or ammonia, allowing for higher density and stability. Both methods aim to enhance hydrogen accessibility for fuel cells and various energy applications.</p></p>
