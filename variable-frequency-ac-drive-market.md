<p><p>Variable Frequency AC Drives (VFDs) are classified into two main market types: low voltage and medium voltage. Low voltage VFDs operate typically below 1,000 volts and are widely used in applications such as fans, pumps, and conveyors, offering energy efficiency and precise motor control. Medium voltage VFDs, on the other hand, operate between 1,000 and 35,000 volts, catering to larger industrial applications like compressors and heavy machinery. Both types enhance operational efficiency, reduce energy consumption, and minimize maintenance costs.</p></p>
