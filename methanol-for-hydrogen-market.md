<p><p>The Methanol for Hydrogen market consists of two primary types: methanol reforming and methanol decomposition. Methanol reforming involves converting methanol into hydrogen and carbon monoxide using heat and catalysts, making it a suitable process for hydrogen production on-demand. In contrast, methanol decomposition directly breaks down methanol into hydrogen and carbon without additional byproducts. Both processes serve as essential methods to generate hydrogen, supporting fuel cell technologies and cleaner energy solutions, while leveraging methanol's high energy density and ease of handling.</p></p>
