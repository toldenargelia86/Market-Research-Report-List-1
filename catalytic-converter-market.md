<p><p>Catalytic converters are emission control devices that reduce harmful pollutants from vehicle exhaust. **Two-way converters** primarily oxidize carbon monoxide (CO) and unburned hydrocarbons (HC) into less harmful gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water (H₂O). **Three-way converters** go further by also reducing nitrogen oxides (NOx) into nitrogen (N₂) and oxygen (O₂), making them more efficient for gasoline engines. The **Other market** includes specialized converters for diesel engines, motorcycles, or industrial equipment, often tailored for specific emission standards or applications. Each type addresses distinct environmental and regulatory needs.</p></p>
