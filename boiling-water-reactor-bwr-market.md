<p><p>A Boiling Water Reactor (BWR) is a type of nuclear reactor that uses water as both a coolant and a neutron moderator. In a BWR, water is boiled directly in the reactor core, generating steam that drives a turbine to produce electricity. BWRs are widely recognized for their safety features and ability to operate at high thermal efficiencies.</p><p>The BWR market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.6% during the forecast period, driven by increasing global energy demands and a shift towards cleaner energy sources.
