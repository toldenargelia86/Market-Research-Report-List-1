<p><ul><li>Independent Automotive Suspension System</li><li>Non-independent Automotive Suspension System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive suspension system market is categorized into independent and non-independent systems. Independent suspension allows each wheel to move independently, enhancing ride comfort, handling, and stability, commonly used in passenger vehicles. Non-independent suspension connects wheels on the same axle, offering durability and cost-effectiveness, often found in heavy-duty and commercial vehicles. Both systems aim to improve vehicle performance, but independent systems prioritize comfort and control, while non-independent systems focus on strength and affordability, catering to different automotive needs.</p></p>
